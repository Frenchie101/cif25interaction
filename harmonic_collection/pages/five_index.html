Colorblindness is a hereditary deficiency that is passed paternally. The condition is caused by a lack of, or malfunction of the cones in the human eye. Rods and Cones, found in the eye's retina are what allow humans to perceive color from ultraviolet light. think of it like a screen, where the cones mix and combine to perceive color, and cones allow us to see black, white, and help convey depth. When the cones in the eye malfunction, the human eye loses its ability to clearly perceive color, leading to colorblindness.
